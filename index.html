Light Sensor

Category: Electronics

Ideation: Sensors are everywhere. As the Internet of Things (IoT) industry booms, sensors will be integrated into almost every electronic device to collect data for machine learning. Therefore, I and my friend decided to build a sensor device for our final project to get a good understanding of sensors. We researched different types of sensor such as ultrasonic transducer, light-dependent resistor, strain gauge, and sound sensor. Finally, we agreed that we are both interested in reducing energy waste from unnecessary lighting. We started drafting the design and sketching our first circuit. And, that was how we got our light meter!

Prototyping: Our light meter has four main components: Wheatstone bridge, difference amplifier, noise filter, and the Arduino control board. Each component was constructed and tested separately to make sure they operated properly before assembling the final circuit. Using a flashlight, the circuit was tested to see if the screen displayed a ‘0’ under ambient luminosity and the screen displayed a ‘9’ under maximum light intensity.

Circuit Design: Teensy Arduino accepts input from 0 to 3.3V, so the job of the difference amplifier stage is to provide gain to reach such voltage levels. The maximum output voltage for the Wheatstone Bridge is around 160mV. It requires the equivalency 3.3V/160mV ≈ 20V/V gain from the amplification stage. Our circuit can also filter noise from the environment or heat emitting from the resistors by passing the signal through the RC Lowpass Filter.

CPU Cooling

Category: Electronics Cooling

The processors in electronic devices process data based on the movement of electronic impulses. When the temperature of the processor rises, the computing speed becomes slower. Improper heat dissipation can eventually make the chip crash and burn. With Moore's Law predicting that processor's speed for computers will double about every 18 months, proper cooling has become essential in the electronics industry. In this project, I will examine how heat sink dissipates heat from the processor to keep the devices operating properly. ( View full analysis )

Ceramic Heat Sink

Category: Electronics Cooling

LED cells have the potential to become the fastest developing lighting technology in different fields of electronics, mechatronics, aerospace, and bio-medical due to their longevity and energy-efficiency. LED cell’s lifespan and efficiency are affected by the heat generated by the cells. To improve the efficiency of LED lighting, a heat sink module is added to the bulb design to dissipate the heat from the cells. In this project, I examined the difference between an Aluminum 6061 heat sink and an N9H – Nishimura Advanced Ceramics heat sink. The result showed that the LED bulb is 18% cooler when using N9H ceramic heat sink instead of Aluminum 6061 heat sink. ( Learn more )
